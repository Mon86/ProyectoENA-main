Supplements

They are food products that are taken orally and whose purpose is to supplement your usual diet.

They are usually concentrates of ingredients intended to complement your diet, such as proteins, amino acids, vitamins, minerals, plant extracts, etc; presented alone or in combination with others to enhance their effectiveness.

They come in different dosages and formats, such as powders, tablets, gels, tablets; as well as in the form of foods such as bars and juices. All food supplements are regulated by law and must be correctly labeled according to current regulations.

It is important to emphasize that all supplement consumption must be integrated into a varied, adequate and complete eating plan and a training program according to the consumer's objective.

Powders are simply concentrated proteins, isolated or a blend, extracted from whey, soy or eggs. They are natural and essential to grow muscles or help them recover.

There are several reasons why any athlete would want to start supplementing their nutrition plan with one of our products:

Are you looking to define and increase muscle mass.

When you are training with a particular objective, such as participating in a marathon.

When you are recovering from an injury.

You are training to get the body you want.

Are you looking to boost your workouts?

You want to have more energy in the day to day to be able to make the most of it

.You are trying to lead a healthier lifestyle and you want to supplement your diet. And many other reasons.

The supplements are available over the counter in pharmacies, dietetics or online, but we always recommend consulting your doctor before any first consumption, especially if you are taking any medication or have any previous medical condition.

It is of vital importance in the decision to purchase dietary, sports and/or food supplements to consume products approved by the corresponding health authority. All products must have the RNE / RNPA National Registry Number.

The RNE/RNPA National Registration Number must appear on the product's label (label) so that you, as a customer, are certain that it is an approved product. This includes products of imported origin, which must have an importer's label that also clarifies the RNE / RNPA.

Although one of the main benefits of protein powders is that they help increase and define muscle mass, this does not imply that the person will gain weight. Proteins provide essential amino acids that plant or animal foods contain, and whose objective is to protect and build muscles as well as favor the creation of other proteins that the body cannot do on its own.

Protein has to be in every healthy and balanced diet, the important thing is knowing how to choose the right sources.

We repeat, the fact of strengthening muscles does not mean gaining weight, but staying healthy and defined if you exercise regularly.

It is always advisable to accompany the consumption of any of these products with a balanced diet and an exercise routine.

The best time of day to consume a supplement varies according to its components and what its benefits are.

They can be consumed: Before training: those intended to improve power and strength in performance. (pre work)

Once the training is finished: since they contribute to the repair of muscles and recovery of nutrients that are lost during exercise. (post work)

Before and after training, as they combine improved performance with improved recovery.

During training: as they replenish energy and help delay fatigue.

The results will depend on each person, their initial physical conditions, their metabolism and also what type of physical activity they do and what their diet is like.
